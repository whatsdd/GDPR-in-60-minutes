# Resources and References

Every page on this site links its source. Here is the same set of sources gathered in one place, so you can bookmark what you need.

## The law itself

- <ArticleRef href="https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj" label="GDPR full text on EUR-Lex" />. Regulation (EU) 2016/679. Switch between 24 EU languages from the same page.
- <ArticleRef href="https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/treaty/char_2012/oj" label="EU Charter of Fundamental Rights" />. Article 8 is the underlying right to data protection.
- <ArticleRef href="https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32002L0058" label="ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC)" />. The cookies and electronic marketing rules that sit on top of the GDPR.

## The regulators

### EU-wide

- <ArticleRef href="https://www.edpb.europa.eu/" label="European Data Protection Board (EDPB)" />. Publishes the guidelines that explain how the GDPR is meant to be applied.
- <ArticleRef href="https://edps.europa.eu/" label="European Data Protection Supervisor (EDPS)" />. Watches over EU institutions themselves.

### National authorities

Each EU member country has its own regulator. The EDPB keeps the full list:

- <ArticleRef href="https://www.edpb.europa.eu/about-edpb/about-edpb/members_en" label="EDPB members list" />

A few that English-speaking readers often need:

- Ireland: <a href="https://www.dataprotection.ie/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Data Protection Commission</a> (lead authority for many US tech firms with EU HQ in Dublin)
- France: <a href="https://www.cnil.fr/en" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">CNIL</a>
- Germany: <a href="https://www.bfdi.bund.de/EN/Home/home_node.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">BfDI</a> (federal, plus one regulator per Land)
- Netherlands: <a href="https://autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl/en" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens</a>
- Spain: <a href="https://www.aepd.es/en" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">AEPD</a>
- UK (separate post-Brexit): <a href="https://ico.org.uk/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ICO</a>

If you cannot find your regulator, search "data protection authority" plus your country name.

## Official guidance worth knowing

These are the EDPB guidelines we cite most often across the modules. Each is short by EU standards (20 to 80 pages).

- Guidelines 3/2018 on the territorial scope of the GDPR (Article 3)
- Guidelines 4/2019 on Article 25 (Data Protection by Design and by Default)
- Guidelines 05/2020 on consent
- Guidelines 07/2020 on the concepts of controller and processor
- Guidelines 01/2022 on data subject rights, right of access
- Guidelines 04/2022 on the calculation of administrative fines
- Guidelines 9/2022 on personal data breach notification
- Guidelines 1/2024 on processing based on legitimate interests
- Recommendations 01/2020 on supplementary measures (the "Schrems II" guidance)

Browse them all: <ArticleRef href="https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/general-guidance/guidelines-recommendations-best-practices_en" label="EDPB guidelines index" />

## Practical tools

- <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">gdpr-info.eu</a>. A nicely cross-linked HTML version of the GDPR text. Handy for quick lookups.
- <a href="https://www.cnil.fr/en/cnils-open-source-pia-software-makes-data-protection-impact-assesment-easier" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">CNIL PIA software</a>. A free desktop tool for running a privacy impact assessment.
- <a href="https://iapp.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">IAPP (International Association of Privacy Professionals)</a>. The main professional body. The certification track (CIPP/E, CIPM, CIPT) is the standard in industry.

## Reading list

- *The EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): A Commentary*, edited by Kuner, Bygrave, Docksey (Oxford University Press). The deep reference.
- *Privacy's Blueprint* by Woodrow Hartzog. Why design choices matter, written for non-lawyers.
- *Data and Goliath* by Bruce Schneier. The bigger picture on surveillance and personal data.

## Stay current

Privacy law moves. Two cheap habits that keep you up to date:

1. Follow Noor's channel: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@GDPRbyN00R" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">@GDPRbyN00R</a>. New videos cover fresh cases and EDPB opinions as they drop.
2. Subscribe to one regulator's newsletter (the ICO and CNIL both have good ones in English).
