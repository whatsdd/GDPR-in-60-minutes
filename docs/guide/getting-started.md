# Start Here

Welcome. This site is the written sidekick to a short YouTube series called **GDPR by Noor**, hosted by [Noor Maryam](https://nmaryam.com). The videos give you the quick story. These pages give you the detail when you want to slow down and read.

## Is this for you?

A short test. Read these three questions. If you answer "yes" to any of them, the GDPR almost certainly applies to your business.

1. Do you keep any information about your customers, users, employees, or website visitors who live in Europe?
2. Do you sell or offer free services to people who live in Europe?
3. Do you track what people in Europe do, for example through your website or app analytics?

If yes, you are in the right place.

::: info Why we wrote it this way
The actual law is 88 pages and uses words like "data subject" and "processing." We translate that to "the person" and "what you do with their info." Same rules, friendlier words.
:::

## The fastest path through this site

If you only have an hour:

1. **Watch the videos in order.** They run about six minutes each, ten in total. Find them on Noor's channel: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@GDPRbyN00R" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">@GDPRbyN00R</a>.
2. **Read [Module 1: Foundations](/modules/01-foundations).** This is the only one you really cannot skip.
3. **Scan the [Glossary](/guide/glossary).** Five minutes. It saves you guesswork later.
4. **Run the [Self-Audit](/guide/self-audit).** Tick the boxes you can. The unticked ones are your to-do list.

If you only have ten minutes, skip to step 4.

## A few things we will keep saying

Three habits will save you most of the trouble:

- **Write things down.** If you make a decision about what to do with people's info, note it. A short paragraph beats a lawyer scrambling through your inbox later.
- **Keep less, not more.** Every extra field of customer data is a future risk. If you do not need it, do not collect it.
- **Tell people the truth.** Privacy notices, cookie banners, breach emails. Plain, honest, on time.
